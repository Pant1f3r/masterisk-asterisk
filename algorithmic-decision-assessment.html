What kind of decision was made about you?

Different denials fall under different federal laws and different enforcement agencies. The first step is identifying which kind of decision you're dealing with.
Credit or Loan Denial
Credit card, personal loan, auto loan, business loan, or other lending. Covered by the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA).
Housing / Rental Denial
Apartment rental, tenant screening, lease renewal, or housing assistance. Covered by the Fair Housing Act and FCRA.
Mortgage Denial
Home loan denial, refinance denial, or unfavorable mortgage terms. Covered by ECOA, FHA, and HMDA reporting requirements.
Employment / Hiring Denial
Job rejection from an algorithmic resume screener, video interview AI, or hiring assessment. Covered by Title VII and (in NYC) Local Law 144.
Insurance Denial / Pricing
Auto, home, health, or life insurance denied or priced unusually high. Covered by state insurance regulators and FCRA.
Healthcare Decision
Treatment denied or care prioritization decision made by an AI system. Covered by Section 1557 of the Affordable Care Act.

Your Legal Right
If you were denied credit, you have a federal right under FCRA §615(a) to receive a written adverse action notice listing the reasons for denial. If you don't have one, the lender owes you one. Same for ECOA Regulation B §1002.9 — the lender must give you specific reasons or notify you that you may request them. Most people don't ask. Ask.
